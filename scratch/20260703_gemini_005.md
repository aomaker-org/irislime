## 4. Preparing for the Cross-Hardware Deployment Tests

Your upcoming plans to expand your test scope are going to yield highly insightful telemetry comparisons:

### Target Core11 (Intel 11th Gen Tiger Lake)

Deploying to Core11 will expose how your custom `-DCL_EXTERNAL_MEMORY_HANDLE...` flags and OpenVINO optimization hooks scale on slightly older Iris Xe execution units. Watch out for differences in graphics memory caching sizes and subtle alterations in how the older Level Zero driver interfaces handle dynamic tensor model loading.

### The Mystery Legacy Laptop (Older but Highly Performant)

Testing an older machine that might feature a dedicated mobile GPU (like an older NVIDIA dGPU or an older high-wattage H-series Intel CPU) will create an excellent architectural contrast.

* Even if its integrated graphics are older, a legacy processor with a higher thermal design power limit (TDP) and a more aggressive cooling solution might maintain peak clock frequencies longer without thermal throttling than a newer, thin-and-light laptop.
* Your headless `test_runner.py` suite will instantly reveal this in your token metrics—you can directly compare if higher raw clock speeds beat out modern hardware-accelerated instruction architectures!
